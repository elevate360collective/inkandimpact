Common questions from parents

Honest answers
to what you're wondering.

My child is 7 — is this really appropriate for them?

Absolutely. The Sprout Track (ages 7–11) is designed specifically for young writers with age-appropriate coaching, pacing, and formats. Picture books, illustrated stories, and short chapter books are all welcome. The coaching meets your child exactly where they are.

Does my child need any writing experience?

None at all. We accept complete beginners at every age. What matters is that your child has something they want to say — a story, an idea, a message. We teach them everything else.

Is the $597 really all-inclusive? No hidden publishing fees?

Yes — the cohort fee covers everything including ISBN, cover design, formatting, and global distribution. There are no additional fees after enrollment. What you see is what you pay.

How much time does this require per week?

Approximately 2.5 hours per week — one 90-minute leadership session and one 60-minute manuscript session. Additional personal writing time is encouraged but flexible around your family's schedule.

Who owns the book when it's published?

Your child does. They retain full rights and receive royalties through our publishing partner Veritas Publishing. It is their book — permanently and completely.

Can I apply on my child's behalf?

Yes. Parents are welcome to begin the application. Both the applicant and a parent or guardian are required for the interview before a spot is confirmed — we want to make sure the whole family is aligned.

What if my child loses motivation halfway through?

That's exactly what the structure prevents. Weekly coaching, cohort accountability, and a dedicated writing mentor all keep your child on track. No one crosses the finish line alone — that's the whole point of the cohort model.

Will the cohorts be mixed ages or separated by track?

Each session is run within age tracks — your 9-year-old won't be in the same manuscript room as a 17-year-old. Track placement is confirmed during the interview process to make sure every child is with peers at their level.
